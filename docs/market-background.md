## 0. Framing (read first)

Stella is not a startup competing with A Place for Mom — it's **the AI product APFM itself should ship.** APFM is the hackathon company (Insight owns a minority stake alongside majority owners General Atlantic and Silver Lake). So the usual "partner vs. compete" question is moot: APFM already owns the demand, the facility relationships, and the placement data. The strategic question is narrower and better — *what is the highest-value AI product APFM can build on top of the demand data only it has?* Stella (facility occupancy & revenue agent) is our answer.

**The moat, in one line:** APFM sees senior-living demand at a scale and granularity no one else does. Turning that demand data into unit-level matching + occupancy planning for the facilities that already pay APFM is a product only APFM can build. *(This is the center of gravity for the pitch.)*

## 1. Market size & structure

- **US senior living market:** ~**$72B in 2025**, projected ~**$76B in 2026** ([Mordor Intelligence](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/united-states-senior-living-market)). Other trackers cite larger figures on broader definitions — treat ~$70–80B as the defensible range.
- **Community/unit universe:** ~**30,600 assisted-living communities** and ~**1.2M licensed AL beds** in the US ([Aline / industry stats](https://alineops.com/blog/assisted-living-statistics/)). "Senior housing" (incl. independent living + memory care) is larger; NIC tracks ~**635,000 occupied units** across its 31 primary markets alone ([NIC](https://www.nic.org/news-press/occupancy-rate-for-senior-living-communities-increased-in-2025-as-construction-stalled/)).
- **Supply is constrained and demand is surging (the tailwind):** units under construction fell to ~**17,000 (3Q25), the lowest since 2012**. NIC estimates the sector needs **200k+ additional units by 2025, 500k by 2028, 775k by 2030** to hold current penetration ([NIC MAP](https://www.nicmap.com/news/senior-living-occupancy-grows-amid-construction-slowdown-limiting-options-for-older-adults/)). Demographics (the 80+ cohort) are the driver.

**Why it matters for Stella:** in a supply-constrained, high-demand market, the bottleneck isn't generating demand — it's *converting and placing it into the right open units.* That's exactly what Stella optimizes.

## 2. Occupancy & pricing backdrop

- **Occupancy:** senior housing ended **2025 at 89.1%**, rose to **89.5% in Q1'26**, expected to pass **90% by end of 2026** — **18 consecutive quarters of increases** ([NIC](https://www.nic.org/news-press/occupancy-rate-for-senior-living-communities-increased-in-2025-as-construction-stalled/); [Senior Housing News](https://seniorhousingnews.com/2026/01/15/high-demand-low-number-of-community-openings-push-senior-living-occupancy-past-89/)).
- **By care type (Q4'25):** Independent Living **>90%**, Assisted Living **87.7%** (AL still has the most room to fill — relevant to Stella's targeting) ([NIC MAP](https://www.nicmap.com/blog/senior-housing-five-key-trends-to-watch-in-2026/)).
- **Monthly rates (2026 national medians):**
  - Assisted living: ~**$5,419** (APFM data) to **$5,900–6,300** (other trackers) ([APFM](https://www.aplaceformom.com/caregiver-resources/articles/cost-of-assisted-living); [SeniorLiving.org](https://www.seniorliving.org/assisted-living/costs/)).
  - Memory care: ~**$6,690** (APFM) to **$7,645–8,019** (US News / SeniorLiving.org). Memory care runs **15–25% above AL** ([SeniorLiving.org](https://www.seniorliving.org/memory-care/costs/)).
  - State spread is wide: AL ranges ~$4,000 to ~$9,600+/mo by state.

## 3. The problem, in the market's own words

- **"Occupancy is the single largest driver of community revenue, and every vacant apartment is lost revenue that can never be recovered."** ([LeadingResponse](https://leadingresponse.com/blog/senior-living-lead-generation-conversion-problem/))
- **It's a conversion problem, not a lead problem:** "Most communities already have enough leads; the real issue is what happens after someone reaches out" ([LeadingResponse](https://leadingresponse.com/blog/senior-living-lead-generation-conversion-problem/)). This is the crux of Stella's wedge — APFM already delivers demand; the leak is downstream.
- **Conversion benchmarks (Aline, 2026):** inquiry→tour ~**29%**; tour→move-in ~**31%** (down from 34%). Top performers hit **35–42%** via faster response and systematic follow-up ([Aline](https://alineops.com/blog/lead-scoring-improve-conversions/); [USR Engage](https://usrengage.com/senior-living-marketing-benchmarks-2026/)).
- **Speed matters enormously:** ~**75% of prospects choose the first community they speak with**; response time over 1 hour bleeds conversions ([USR Engage](https://usrengage.com/senior-living-marketing-benchmarks-2026/)).
- **Cost per move-in:** ~**$2,400 (IL) to $4,600 (memory care)**, *excluding* referral-agency fees; sales cycle **70–120 days** ([USR Engage](https://usrengage.com/senior-living-marketing-roi-guide/)).

## 4. A Place for Mom — the demand layer

- **Model:** two-sided referral marketplace. Families get free advisory placement help; **communities pay APFM a fee only on a successful move-in.** Fees commonly run **50%–100%+ of the first month's rent**, ~**$3,000–$6,000 per placement** ([search synthesis; verify exact fee]).
- **Scale:** **15,000+ vetted communities/providers** in the referred network; **17,000+ in the database**; national advisor force ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Place_for_Mom); APFM).
- **Ownership:** majority owned by **General Atlantic + Silver Lake** (equal stakes, since 2017, bought from Warburg Pincus); **Insight Partners took a minority stake** in the Jan 2022 **$175M** raise that valued APFM at **>$1B**. CEO Sean Kell ([PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/a-place-for-mom-announces-strategic-investment-from-general-atlantic-and-silver-lake-300483582.html); [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Place_for_Mom)).
- **Revenue:** third-party estimates vary wildly (~$620M to ~$2.7B) and are **unreliable** — do not cite a hard revenue number in the pitch. *(Flag as unverified.)*
- **Recent strategic direction (Nov 2025):** APFM rebranded and is **leaning into AI** — prioritizing direct connections in AI tools like ChatGPT/Gemini and expanding home care ([APFM press](https://www.aplaceformom.com/about/news-and-press-releases/a-place-for-mom-ceo-weve-built-a-marketing-tech-machine-to-power-senior-living-move-ins)). **This is a tailwind for the pitch** — Stella is consistent with a strategy APFM has already publicly committed to.
- **Business-model expansion (the APFM upside):** today APFM earns a one-time per-referral fee. Stella adds a **recurring, per-community SaaS line** for occupancy forecasting, lead prioritization, pricing/concession recommendations, and capacity benchmarking — deepening the facility relationship and expanding TAM beyond the placement event.

## 5. Competitive landscape & whitespace

The stack: **demand (APFM) → [missing matching/occupancy layer] → community capacity (PMS/CRM).** Incumbents own the ends; nobody owns the connection anchored on APFM demand.

| Player | What they are | What they do | What they DON'T do | 
|---|---|---|---|
| **PointClickCare** | Senior-care/post-acute OS; **valued >$5B** (H&F + JMI + Dragoneer) ([PE Insights](https://pe-insights.com/pointclickcares-value-rises-to-5bn-hellman-friedman-and-jmi-equity/)) | System of record: clinical, resident, billing across skilled nursing / senior living / home health | No demand-side matching; no APFM demand data |
| **Aline** (Enquire+Sherpa+Glennis, merged) | Market-leading senior-living CRM; **~5,700 communities, ~800 customers, 50 of top 150 operators** ([PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/enquire-glennis-solutions-and-sherpa-crm-join-forces-to-create-market-leading-senior-living-software-platform-301661438.html)) | CRM + "Aline Intelligence": lead scoring, conversion/readiness prediction | Owns the CRM, not the demand; scores leads it's given, isn't wired to APFM's demand pipe |
| **Prorize** | Dynamic-pricing point solution | Senior Living Rent Optimizer (unit-level pricing) since ~2017 ([Prorize](https://www.prorize.com/senior-living/)) | Pricing only; no demand matching or lead prioritization |
| **Yardi / RealPage** | Multifamily-rooted PMS + revenue mgmt with senior-living modules | Pricing/occupancy analytics | Generic RM; RealPage under antitrust cloud (see §7) |
| **SmarterCare** | Senior-care software (team-flagged) | *(verify offering)* | — |

**Feature-vs-company answer:** incumbents *could* bolt pieces of this on — Aline is closest — but none of them own **APFM's proprietary demand data**, which is the input that makes unit-level matching work. That's APFM's structural right-to-win and the reason this is a product, not a feature. *(This is the argument to make on the moat slide.)*

## 6. ROI — illustrative, assumptions labeled

Core logic (team's framing): **~2–3 points of occupancy uplift × average monthly rate.**

Illustrative single community *(assumptions in brackets — adjust freely):*
- [100-unit community], [avg rate $5,800/mo] → **1 occupancy point ≈ 1 unit ≈ $5,800/mo ≈ ~$70k/yr** in recurring revenue.
- **2–3 points ≈ ~$140k–$210k/yr** in incremental revenue per community.
- Against a plausible Stella SaaS price of [$15k–$40k/community/yr], that's a **~4–14x return** for the community — easy to justify.
- Scaled across APFM's [15,000-community] network at [modest penetration], this is a **large recurring ARR line** on top of referral fees. *(All bracketed inputs are assumptions for a toggleable model, not sourced facts.)*

Supporting real benchmarks to anchor the story: vacancy is unrecoverable lost revenue; tour→move-in sits at ~31% with top performers at 35–42% (so a few points of conversion lift is realistic); 75% of prospects pick whoever responds first (so better prioritization directly converts).

## 7. Regulatory guardrails (design constraints, not blockers)

- **RealPage / DOJ (housing antitrust):** DOJ settled with RealPage in late 2025; **~$360M** in total settlements and a **3-year monitor**, and RealPage was **barred from using nonpublic, competitively sensitive competitor data to recommend pricing** ([ProPublica](https://www.propublica.org/article/doj-realpage-settlement-rental-price-fixing-case); [Multifamily Dive](https://www.multifamilydive.com/news/realpage-settlement-algorithmic-pricing/820745/)). Senior living is housing. **Design rule:** Stella prices off a community's *own* data + public signals only — never pooled competitor pricing.
- **Fair Housing:** ranking/prioritizing prospective residents by budget or likelihood must be auditable and non-discriminatory. **Design rule:** lead prioritization logic is transparent and defensible, not a black box.
- **Referral-practice scrutiny:** APFM has faced a **Senate probe** over referral practices ([NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/senate-announces-probe-place-for-mom-referral-service-rcna157282)) — a reason to keep recommendations resident-benefit-aligned, not purely revenue-maximizing.

*One slide of "we've thought about the risk" (own-data pricing, auditable lead logic) turns a potential objection into a credibility signal.*
